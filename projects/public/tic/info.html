Tic-Tac-Toe
Synopsis
Tic-Tac-Toe is a game with very simple rules and is most frequently played among those of younger age. The game takes two players who each take turns placing their designated symbol on a 3x3 grid (X or O). The first person to mark three of their symbols in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row is declared the winner.
While the game can be enjoyed without strategy, there are ways to ensure your victory. With optimal play, the game will always end in a draw, but a small mistake from either player could destroy their chance of winning.
History
The most early traces we have of this game are from Ancient Egypt, where they played with roofing tiles (1300 BCE). There were also variations in the games terni lapilli from Ancient Rome, three men's morris from certain parts of Asia, and finally, the closest ancestor, the British noughts and crosses.
